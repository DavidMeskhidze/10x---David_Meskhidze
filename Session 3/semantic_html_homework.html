I wanted to show my judo self always, like my Tokyo Games self. I wanted to show people that my judo style is complete and do so by winning the Games.

Shohei Ono, the two-time Olympic gold medalist in judo, stepped down from competition following the Tokyo 2020 Games. After years of dedicated training and intense competition, the legendary judoka has found a new rhythm in life, trading the mat for the classroom.

Ono has temporarily relocated to Scotland, where he is immersing himself in English language studies. He is also actively involved in sharing his knowledge and experience, conducting judo classes and seminars both within Scotland and internationally.

Perhaps enough time has passed to reflect. How does Shohei Ono feel about his retirement? What are his thoughts on his illustrious career? What does the future hold for the man who once reigned supreme on the judo mat?
